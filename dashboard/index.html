Core Findings

Five empirically validated conclusions across 3 analytical layers

Finding 1
RQ1 · RQ2

AI is a real but minority driver

AI-attributed layoffs rose 1,100%+ YoY but represent only 4.7% of total cuts. Post-pandemic correction and macroeconomic pressures dominate.

Finding 2
RQ1

AI-Washing distorts the picture

An estimated 15–25% of AI-cited layoffs lack sufficient causality evidence. Class C firms show no statistically significant AI CapEx → Layoff link (p=0.23).

Finding 3
RQ3

Early-career workers most vulnerable

Workers aged 22–25 in high-AI-exposure roles face a 13–16% relative employment decline — a structural narrowing no policy framework currently addresses.

Finding 4
RQ4

Augmentation is a strategic choice

83% of firms with AI-driven productivity gains did NOT reduce headcount (EY 2025). Displacement is management discretion, not technological inevitability.

Finding 5
RQ6

Transition costs are the real risk

WEF projects a net +78M jobs by 2030, but geographic and skill mismatches between displaced and created roles create a first-order policy crisis.

Objective 5 — Policy Gaps & Recommendations

Current framework adequacy scores and evidence-based recommendations (§8.4). Addresses RQ4.

Policy Adequacy Radar

Current vs. required adequacy across 5 critical policy domains

Evidence-Based Policy Recommendations

🔴 CRITICAL

Mandatory AI Disclosure in Restructuring

SEC and labour ministry filings must include standardised AI causality evidence requirements for any restructuring citing AI as a driver. Eliminates AI-washing loophole.

Target: 0% → 80% Adequacy
🔴 CRITICAL

Early-Career Entry Point Protection

New policy framework specifically for ages 22–25 in high-AI-exposure sectors: targeted subsidies, AI-native apprenticeships, university pipeline partnerships.

Target: 5% → 70% Adequacy
🔴 CRITICAL

AI-Washing Detection & Enforcement

Independent audit body to verify AI causality claims in corporate restructuring filings, with penalties for misrepresentation. Aligns with LSE findings.

Target: 0% → 75% Adequacy
🟠 HIGH

Scaled Reskilling Infrastructure

48% of employees identify training as most critical AI adoption factor. Government programs must reach 10× current scale. EY: augmentation firms outperform displacement firms.

Target: 35% → 80% Adequacy
🟠 HIGH

G20 AI Employment Coordination Framework

AI restructuring is global; policy responses remain national. An international coordination mechanism is urgently needed before 2027 when MIT headroom begins to close.

Target: 10% → 60% Adequacy

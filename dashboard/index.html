RQ5 — The Investment Paradox

Is there a measurable correlation between AI investment scale and concurrent workforce reductions? Does it vary by sector?

AI Investment vs. Total Layoffs — Scatter (12 Firms)

Pearson r = 0.78 — strong positive correlation confirmed

Sectoral Correlation Heatmap

AI CapEx ratio ↔ Layoff rate correlation by sector

Sector Portfolio: Total AI Investment vs. Total Layoffs

SectorTotal AI Investment ($M)Total LayoffsAvg CapEx RatioAvg Layoff RateCorrelation
RQ5 Finding: The Pearson correlation between total AI investment and total layoffs across the 12-firm panel is r = 0.78, confirming the "Investment Paradox" — firms that invest most in AI also cut the most workers. However, sector-level analysis reveals significant heterogeneity: Enterprise Software (r ≈ 0.81) and Logistics (r ≈ 0.75) show stronger links than Media/Advertising (r ≈ 0.22), where AI-washing is more prevalent. This is consistent with RQ1's finding that genuine AI restructuring (Class A/B) drives measurable displacement, while narrative-driven AI-washing (Class C) inflates correlation noise.
